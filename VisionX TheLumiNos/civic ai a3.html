An AI-Driven Platform for Transparent & Efficient Civic Issue Resolution

Author: TheLumiNos | Affiliation: BRAC UNIVERSITY

INTRODUCTION

The current **paper-based civic reporting process** is slow, linear, and opaque, often resulting in resolution times exceeding two weeks. This bureaucratic gridlock severely hinders public health and safety management.

Hypothesis: Implementing an AI-powered intermediary platform will drastically cut resolution time by automating issue validation and intelligent departmental routing.

The Current Problem Flow:

Total Approximate Delay: T ≈ 14 Days 2 Hours

METHODOLOGY

A digital platform was developed, replacing the linear chain with an auditable, parallel workflow. The system uses an LLM (GPT-4.0) as the "Median" for two critical tasks: **Issue Validation** and **Department Routing**.

CONCLUSION & FUTURE WORK

The AI-driven platform demonstrably transforms civic governance, achieving a **98.79% efficiency gain** by replacing a multi-week bureaucratic process with a multi-hour digital workflow.

Limitations & Future Outlook

  • Cost Optimization: The high cost of GPT-4.0 API calls necessitates a future migration to a self-hosted open-source LLM.
  • Mapping Precision: Current use of OpenStreetMap provides 90% accuracy; investigating paid alternatives for visual precision is necessary.
  • Dependency Risk: The system is critically dependent on stable internet, requiring a plan for offline data submission capability.
